Antlers

Antlers are bony outgrowths from an animalâ€™s skull found in members of the Cervidae family. These structures are composed of bone, cartilage, fibrous tissue, skin, nerves, and blood vessels. Unlike horns, antlers are not covered by a keratin sheath, and can be shed and regrown each year. They are also branched, and are usually found on males. The only exception are the female reindeer/caribou, which have smaller antlers than their male counterparts. (Wikipedia, nps and bovids of the world).
