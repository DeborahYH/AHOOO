Horns

By Syncerus caffer

April 12, 2024

Horns are paired structures which protrude from certain animal’s skulls. They consist of a core made of live bone, which is covered by a keratinized sheath. Unlinke antlers, these structures are permanent - growing throughout an animal’s life - and do not branch. Horns also vary from between species in shape and size.[1,2]

Function

Animals can use their horns for defending themselves, or fighting other member of the same species. This occurs when the animals fight for territory, mating priority, or foraging rights [3,4]. Some species also use their horns as displays in courtship, or use them to root in the soil or strip bark from trees.

Apart from those uses, horns can also help animals regulate their temperature – the blood vessels found in theses structures help them exchange heat with the environment. [3]
